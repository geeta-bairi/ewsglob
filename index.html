Blacksmithy

Blacksmithy is one of the oldest shaping processes used for producing articles where size and accuracy are not important. The parts are shaped by heating them in an open fire and applying compressive forces using a hammer. It consists essentially of altering the shape and section of metal by hammering at a temperature of about 980 degrees. Almost all metal alloys can be forged. The low and medium carbon steels are readily hot forged without difficulty but the high carbon and alloy steels, wrought iron, copper-base alloys, aluminium alloys, and magnesium alloys are also forged. Stainless steels, nickel-based superalloys, and titanium are forged especially for aerospace uses.
